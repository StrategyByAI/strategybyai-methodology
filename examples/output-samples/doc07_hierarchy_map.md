## 28 Entities Across Four Threat Levels

### Enemy Zone (4 entities — 14% of total, 100% of existential risk)

```
Entity                      Status    Trajectory    Current%  Recommended%
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Lab-Grown Diamond Producers  ENEMY     ESCALATING     <10%      40–50%
Anglo American PLC           ENEMY     ACTIVE          60%      15%
Transparency Platforms       ENEMY     ESCALATING      <5%      40–50%
Government of Botswana       ENEMY*    ESCALATING      25%      40–50%

* Escalating from ADVERSARY to ENEMY. Transition projected Q4 2026.
```

### Critical Finding: Capacity Mathematics

```
Each enemy requires 40–50% organisational capacity.
4 enemies × 40–50% = 160–200% total requirement.
De Beers has 100% total capacity.
Anglo American consumes 60%.
Effective available for strategic use: 40%.

Capacity deficit: 120–160%.
```

### Resource Misallocation

```
Anglo American:       60% actual  vs  15% recommended  → OVER-ALLOCATED (4×)
Luxury competitors:   38% actual  vs  21–32% rec.      → OVER-ALLOCATED
Lab-Grown Producers:  <10% actual vs  40–50% rec.      → CRITICALLY UNDER
Transparency:         <5% actual  vs  40–50% rec.      → CRITICALLY UNDER
Botswana:             25% actual  vs  40–50% rec.      → UNDER
```

The organisation spends four times the recommended resources managing its parent company while spending one-quarter to one-tenth the recommended resources on the enemies that will determine whether the company survives.

### Adversary Zone (8 entities — 29% of total)

Tiffany, Cartier, Harry Winston, Blue Nile, Luxury Conglomerates, Private Equity bidders, Signet, Chinese Market dynamics. Significant but survivable confrontations.

### Rival Zone (9 entities — 32% of total)

Van Cleef, Bulgari, Chopard, Graff, Pandora, Independent Producers, Alternative Luxury, Other Gemstones, Industrial Synthetics. Third-party competition with limited existential impact.

### Alien Zone (7 entities — 25% of total)

Blockchain platforms, Synthetic Biology, Circular Economy, AI Design/Manufacturing, Cryptocurrency culture, De-Growth movement, Quantum computing applications. Latent threats with 3–10 year emergence timelines.

---

**What this demonstrates:** The methodology doesn't produce a "competitive landscape overview." It produces a hierarchy that reveals the structural impossibility of current resource allocation — a finding invisible to conventional competitive analysis.

**How it feeds forward:** The 160–200% capacity requirement defines Module Four's situation reading. The resource misallocation finding drives Module Five's strategy: concentrate on ultra-luxury where fewer enemies operate at full strength, reducing the effective capacity requirement to manageable levels.
