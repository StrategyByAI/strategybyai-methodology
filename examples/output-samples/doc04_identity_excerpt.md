## Key Finding: Complete Mission-History-Doctrine Incoherence

### Coherence Matrix

| Integration Dimension | Status | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Mission–History | Complete misalignment | Mission requires capabilities history didn't develop |
| History–Doctrine | No doctrine / mislearned lessons | Strategic mistakes repeated (Lightbox replicated Canadian failure 27 years later) |
| Doctrine–Mission | Contradictory implicit doctrines | Strategic paralysis — organisation cannot commit to consistent direction |
| Three-Way Integration | Fundamental incoherence | Organisational schizophrenia — stakeholders operating incompatible frameworks |

### Three Contradictory Implicit Doctrines

1. **Legacy monopoly doctrine:** "Maintain control" — impossible in competitive markets
2. **Defensive brand doctrine:** "Protect premium" — underfunded ($20M vs $200M+ needed)
3. **Financial optimisation doctrine:** "Maximise cash" — contradicts brand building

No explicit doctrine exists. De Beers oscillates between these three depending on which leader is in charge.

### Critical Finding

> De Beers UK currently lacks authentic integrated strategic identity. Organisation exhibits identity crisis where mission aspirations, historical constraints, and doctrinal absence create strategic vacuum. Without ownership transition providing mission-aligned capital and strategic continuity, identity integration impossible within timeframes competitive dynamics allow.
